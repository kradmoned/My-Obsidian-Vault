The **complement of an event** $E$, which is denoted by $\mathbf{E'}$, is defined as **the set of all outcomes in the sample space that are not included in event $E$**.

### The Complementary Events Rule

The concept of the complement is integral to the **Range of Probabilities Rule** and is governed by the following relationship:

*   The sum of the probability of event $E$ and the probability of its complement, $P(E')$, must always equal 1.
    $$\mathbf{P(E) + P (E' ) = 1}$$
*   This relationship allows for the calculation of either probability by using the other:
    $$\mathbf{P(E) = 1 – P (E' )}$$
    $$\mathbf{P (E' ) = 1 – P(E)}$$

### Example

An example illustrating the use of the complement involves selecting a chip from a basket containing 5 red chips, 4 blue chips, and 6 white chips.

If the event $E$ is "selecting a blue chip," then $P(E) = \frac{4}{15} \approx 0.267$.

The complement, $E'$, is "not selecting a blue chip". The probability of the complement is calculated using the rule:
$$P (\text{not selecting a blue chip}) = 1 – P (\text{selecting a blue chip})$$
$$P (E') = 1 - \frac{4}{15} = \frac{11}{15} \approx 0.733$$
